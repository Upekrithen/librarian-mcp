## SECTION 4: THREE-GEAR CURRENCY SYSTEM

### Credits
- Exchange rate: $1 = 1 Credit
- **One-Way Valve:** Credits NEVER cash out to fiat currency
- Used for marketplace transactions
- Per-person cap: $5,000

### Marks
- Earned through effort and contribution (not purchased)
- Effort-differential currency
- **One-way ratchet:** value only goes up, never down
- All Marks are backed
- Used to reward platform contribution beyond direct commerce

### Joules
- Surplus/"forever stamp" currency
- Represents stored value from excess production
- The third gear — handles what Credits and Marks don't cover

### Why Three Currencies Don't Create Arbitrage
Each serves a distinct function and they don't compete. WIR Bank (Switzerland, founded 1934, 92+ years of operation) has proven multi-currency cooperative systems work. The Marks backing ratchet prevents devaluation. The one-way valve on Credits prevents extraction.
