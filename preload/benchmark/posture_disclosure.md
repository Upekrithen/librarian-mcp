# Posture Disclosure — Required Verbatim

**What this is:** A verbatim disclosure statement that must appear in any publication, benchmark write-up, letter, or op-ed that reports R10 Cross-Vendor Benchmark results and includes OpenAI models. This is a Founder-ratified transparency rule.

---

## The disclosure (verbatim)

> We include OpenAI in this study despite substantive concerns about their governance trajectory, because a cross-vendor study that excludes the market leader is not a cross-vendor study. Measurement is the contribution; endorsement is not conveyed by inclusion.

---

## Why it exists

The Eyewitness Benchmark (R10) measures eight models across four vendors: Anthropic, Google, OpenAI, Perplexity. Including OpenAI is methodologically necessary — excluding the market leader would render the "cross-vendor" framing meaningless.

Excluding OpenAI for values-based reasons, however defensible, would also give critics a legitimate angle: "your benchmark is cherry-picked to exclude the model you compete with." The result would no longer be credible as a measurement.

**But including OpenAI without comment implies endorsement of their current governance.** That's untrue. Liana Banyan has documented, ongoing concerns about OpenAI's governance trajectory — the same concerns that animate much of the platform's open-source + cooperative architecture choices.

The disclosure resolves the tension: *we measure them because we have to for the measurement to be honest; we disclose the tension because pretending it isn't there would be dishonest.*

---

## Where to use it

- **Academic papers** citing R10 results: include verbatim as a footnote or posture-disclosure section.
- **Op-eds** citing R10 results: include verbatim or paraphrase *clearly* (do not drop it silently).
- **Social posts** citing R10 results: link to the full benchmark page; posture disclosure must be one click away.
- **Letters** citing R10 results: include if substantive discussion of the benchmark; can be omitted if only a single number is cited (e.g., "we saw 86.1pp lift cross-vendor" without naming OpenAI specifically).

---

## Where NOT to modify

**Do not soften the disclosure.** Versions like "despite minor concerns" or "we acknowledge differences with OpenAI's approach" dilute the tension the disclosure is designed to carry. The language is deliberate: "substantive concerns," "governance trajectory," "not conveyed by inclusion." These phrases are load-bearing.

**Do not expand the disclosure with a list of specific OpenAI grievances.** That turns the benchmark paper into an OpenAI critique paper, which it isn't. The disclosure names the tension and lets the reader find their own sources on governance concerns.

---

## Canonical precedent

This disclosure was Founder-authored B111 when the Eyewitness Benchmark cross-vendor table was locked (Apr 20–21, 2026). It is the first example of a named posture-disclosure rule in the Liana Banyan publishing discipline.

Similar disclosures may be added over time — when a specific methodological choice requires naming a tension we are not resolving in the current paper. Each such disclosure follows the same shape:

1. Name the choice.
2. Name why the choice was necessary.
3. Name the tension that remains.
4. Refuse to let the choice imply endorsement.
