# The Three-Clock Timeline

**What this is:** Canonical timeline for the Liana Banyan platform. Three distinct clocks measure three distinct things — collapsing them into one clock either overstates hype ("just 6 months!") or understates work ("40 years in the making"). Both are wrong; together they're right.

---

## Clock 1 — The Problem Clock

**Duration:** 40+ years, and counting.

The problems Liana Banyan addresses — concentration of economic surplus, platform extraction, gatekeeping of entrepreneurship, opacity of margin, systemic disenfranchisement of people living outside concentrated capital — have been accelerating since at least the 1980s. The Founder was 17 when expelled from college on Christmas Eve 1992 and slept in a Jeep. Poordom has been the Founder's lived context for 53 years. The Problem Clock is older than the Platform Clock and the Build Clock combined.

---

## Clock 2 — The Founder Preparation Clock

**Duration:** Decade-plus, continuous.

The Founder has been preparing to build *this specific platform* for over a decade. Components: 21 years IT development, FAA Commercial Rotary Wing IFR rating (Aviation 15A), Infantry 11B + OCS, chess top 0.4% globally (2080s rating), shape-note singing, seminary laptop-notes precedent, a saxophone he took from 13th chair to 1st (see `anachronism_principle.md`). The Anachronism Principle — that the way you learned things affected *whether* you learned them — is the axiomatic form. The Preparation Clock looks like autobiography; it's actually design-method formation.

Started explicitly in 1989 (37 years ago at time of platform launch).

---

## Clock 3 — The Build Clock

**Duration:** Six months, dense.

The actual platform build began approximately November 2025 and reached launch on April 12, 2026. Inside this 6-month window: 36 live production systems, 13 provisional patents (2,412 formal claims), 2,267 innovations, 189 Puddings, 41 papers, 95 letters staged, 706+ Spoonfuls, 584 BST episodes, the R9 memory system, the Librarian MCP public repo, the Eyewitness Benchmark (R10) cross-vendor replication. AI engineering team: Rook (Gemini), Knight (Cursor), Bishop (Claude), Pawn (Perplexity). Founder = AI Tuner.

## How to apply

- **For skeptical audiences** (press, investors, regulators, patent office): lead with all three clocks. Problem is 40+ years old. Preparation is decade-plus. Build is 6 months *on top of the preparation*. The short clock is only short because the long clocks prepared the ground.

- **For fast-pitch audiences** (HN launches, social posts): lead with the Build Clock. Receipt of "36 systems in 6 months" lands faster than the preparation story. The preparation story earns the second read.

- **Do not lead with the Problem Clock alone** in material targeting younger audiences — "40 years of X is broken" reads as fatigue rather than qualification. Pair it with one of the other clocks to convert fatigue into earned perspective.

- **For academic audiences:** the Preparation Clock is the load-bearing one. The Anachronism Principle is an academic-legible claim; the 6-month Build Clock is not.

---

## Common drift modes

- **Collapsing to one clock.** Anyone who says "the platform was built in 6 months" without naming the preparation clock has flattened the story. Counter with: "The build was 6 months; the preparation was decades."

- **Collapsing the preparation clock.** Saying "the Founder has been working on this for 37 years" conflates problem-awareness with product-work, which is false. The preparation was cross-domain life-work that only became product-work in the last 6 months. Three clocks, not one very long clock.

- **Reducing the problem clock to a crisis.** "AI is eating everything" as the framing makes the problem look recent. It's not. The problem clock has been running since at least the 1980s economic restructuring; AI is the latest accelerant, not the cause.
