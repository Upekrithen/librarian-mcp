# The Anachronism Principle — Design Method

**What this is:** The Founder's design method, named B110. *An ancient, disciplined practice you personally learned teaches a specific mode of modern structured thinking, and you reach for that practice when you design.*

**Founder's axiomatic form:**
> *"The point is, I think the way I learned things affected WHETHER I learned them."*

This is Keystone #13. It's the conditional that explains Founder's cross-domain fluency.

---

## Known instantiations (biographical)

These are the Founder-provided instances as of B110. More are expected as they surface.

1. **Shape-note singing → Librarian / R9 architecture.** Shape-note is constrained canonical notation. Teaches: ordered decoding under constraint. Applied in LB as the Librarian's tightly-curated memory packet and the Cooperative Defensive Patent Pledge's canonical-truth-first discipline.

2. **Aviation (IFR-rated 15A) → Triple Redundant Verification (#2263).** Aviation teaches: tolerance-stack reasoning, redundancy-first design, margin-of-error discipline. Applied as Triple Redundant Verification, the Scrambler, and the No-Unwitnessed-AI-Output canonical law.

3. **Saxophone (HS sophomore year) → persistence-despite-non-standard-background.** Mr. Garcia (band director) told the Founder that because he only knew shape notes (not standard notation), success was unlikely. Founder borrowed an alto sax, barely opened the case over summer, practiced embouchure with the reed/mouthpiece like a kazoo. Started at 13th chair. Rose to 4th. In public test, took **1st chair** from Bryan. **Lesson: non-standard preparation doesn't preclude standard-setting results; and being told you can't is sometimes the recruiting signal.**

4. **Typing (Typing 1 → Typing 2 transition, HS freshman → sophomore) → discontinuous-improvement.** Last speed test of Typing 1: 15 wpm. First day of Typing 2 first test: 35 wpm. Later that week: 55 wpm. Stayed above 50 until seminary. **Lesson: discontinuous improvement comes from method-change, not practice-volume; absence of hardware forced absence of bad habits.**

5. **1997 internet argument with Dad → tech-adoption foresight.** Dad said "www" was an interesting toy but would never be useful for business. Founder said the future was upon us. Made first website for his own companies (Red Oak Printing / Night Owl Web Design) in 1997. **Lesson: correct-on-future doesn't require being correct-on-tactics.**

6. **Seminary laptop notes (Southwest School of Bible Studies — 2-year 300-credit program, 7 daily classes) → durable-infrastructure-over-discard-workflow.** Founder was the first student to take laptop notes across all 7 daily classes. Told faculty he could **reference the notes for the rest of his life** instead of digging through papers he'd never revisit. **Lesson: choose durable infrastructure over discard workflows, even when it's not standard — especially when it's not standard.**

7. **Mr. Cloyd / Sears Lewistown layaway → The Cloyd Pattern.** See `cloyd_pattern.md`. Economic-trust-before-earned → labor-repaid-forward. Ancient small-town layaway discipline teaches modern cooperative credit-extension design.

8. **Military (Pre-BASIC with Cory → Infantry 11B → OCS → Aviation 15A) → follow-through-despite-peer-path-divergence.** Pre-BASIC planning session with a peer (Cory) who decided not to sign. Founder proceeded. **Lesson: you decide based on your path, not on whether the person who walked in with you walks out with you.**

*Founder comment B110:* "We have, oh, a LOT of examples of it. :D" — expect more as they surface.

## How to apply

- **Recruitment filter.** "What ancient disciplined practice trained your modern thinking?" reveals the hire's design cognition. Practitioners of martial arts, orchestral musicianship, liturgical tradition, classical languages, celestial navigation, masonry, cabinetry, classical dance, chess, etc. all answer usefully. People who can't answer probably aren't Crown-Letter-level hires.

- **Essay candidate.** Short-form Pudding on "why I keep reaching for shape-note when I design." Pairs naturally with Keystones #3 and #4 ("potatoes at the hoe handle" + "two suits").

- **Paired Provenance anchor.** The Anachronism Principle IS the ethical/biographical twin for the Librarian + R9 + #2263 architecture cluster. Already practiced; now named.

- **Design-review question.** On any new LB feature: "what ancient discipline is this reaching for, and does the design respect what that discipline actually taught?" Catches inventions-disguised-as-innovation.
