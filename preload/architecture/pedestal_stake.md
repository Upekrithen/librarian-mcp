# Pedestal Stake / Upekrithen LLC — The Seller of Record

**What this is:** The legal vehicle through which outside investors acquire exposure to Liana Banyan Corporation. Upekrithen, LLC (Wyoming, 100% owned by Liana Banyan Corporation) is the Seller of Record for Pedestal Stakes.

Named this way deliberately: *pedestal* = the named individual contribution position. A Pedestal Stake represents participation in that individual contributor's pool.

---

## Why Upekrithen, not the Corporation directly

- **Separates platform commerce from investor capital.** Platform commerce (membership, creator transactions, Cost+20% margin) runs through Liana Banyan Corporation. Investor exposure runs through Upekrithen LLC. Clean ledger, clean audit, clean SEC posture.

- **Preserves the zero-silent-share IP distribution.** Patent revenue flows via the 60/20/10/10 split (see `ip_load_balancing.md`). The platform does not extract from patent revenue. Investor return instead comes through Pedestal Stakes paid out of a separate pool managed by Upekrithen.

- **Howey-consciousness.** The platform's Credits are structured to fail Howey prong 3 (non-cashout, no expectation of profit). Pedestal Stakes are *securities-adjacent* by design — Upekrithen handles them under the correct regulatory framework, keeping platform-member Credits cleanly outside securities classification.

---

## Stake structure

- **Per-stake payout cap:** $10,000,000 over the life of the stake.
- **SAA influence cap per member:** $10,000,000 cumulative. Overflow cascades to members they've worked with (Patrons engaged, Members Patroned, Ripples backed). Cascade recurses generationally.
- **Not common equity.** Pedestal Stakes are participation rights in specific contributor pools, not fractional ownership of the Corporation.
- **Time-bound.** Individual stakes have defined terms. Pattern: 20-year window for the Herjavec RPA (half of Founder-creator slice). Other stake structures vary by pool.

---

## Relationship to the IP split

The 60/20/10/10 IP distribution (see `ip_load_balancing.md`) governs **patent revenue distribution**. Pedestal Stakes are funded **separately**, through Upekrithen. A Pedestal Stake-holder does NOT automatically receive a share of patent revenue; that's governed by the 60/20/10/10 split and member votes on bucket allocation.

Two distinct revenue architectures serving two distinct purposes:
- **Patent revenue (60/20/10/10):** how contributions are rewarded and how the member-voted patent buckets fund themselves.
- **Pedestal Stakes (Upekrithen):** how outside investors gain exposure to platform success without distorting the member-governance structure.

## How to apply

- **In investor conversations:** Upekrithen is what investors actually buy. Liana Banyan Corporation is not for sale. The Corporation is member-governed and its constitutional locks (Cost+20%, DNA Lock, 60/20/10/10, $10M cap) make hostile acquisition mathematically infeasible. Investors participate via Upekrithen.

- **In cooperative conversations:** the Upekrithen architecture is what allows the Corporation to be a cooperative *without* cutting itself off from capital that wants to participate. Cooperatives that refuse outside capital on principle often stagnate. Cooperatives that admit outside capital directly lose the cooperative. Upekrithen is the third path.

- **In SEC / regulator conversations:** clean separation is the argument. Credits never classified as securities (one-way valve, Howey prong-3 fail). Pedestal Stakes classified correctly under Upekrithen. The Corporation itself has no common equity and no tradeable shares.

---

## Canonical rule

- Upekrithen is for Upekrithen ONLY — meaning, the Pedestal Stake vehicle is *not* how the Corporation itself raises capital. Liana Banyan Corporation does not take VC. It does not offer common equity. Its member-governance architecture is constitutional and non-tradeable.
