# IP Load Balancing — The 60/20/10/10 Distribution

**What this is:** The constitutional rule for how patent revenue is distributed inside Liana Banyan. The platform retains **zero silent share** — all operational funding comes from Pedestal sales through Upekrithen LLC.

---

## The Split

| Slice | Destination | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **60%** | Patent buckets (member-voted) | Members vote on which buckets get funded. The majority slice goes where the people who actually use the platform choose to put it. |
| **20%** | Founder-creator | Compensation for the originator of each innovation. Half of this slice (10% of total) is the Herjavec RPA — 20 years, capped at $10M. |
| **10%** | Global sponsor pool | Shared pool for contributors who helped without being the named originator. |
| **10%** | Individual Pedestals | Direct attribution to named individual contributors per innovation. |

**Anti-drift rule:** Older platform documents saying "60/20/20" are STALE ON SIGHT. The current split is 60/20/10/10.

---

## Why this split

- **Anti-concentration.** Prevents winner-takes-all from re-emerging inside the platform — the very disease the platform was built to cure. Pairs with the **$10M Influence Cap** (any member's cumulative SAA is capped at $10M; overflow cascades to members they've worked with, recursively).

- **Founder slice is NOT majority.** The Founder takes 20%. Sixty percent goes to member-voted buckets. This is the governance signal: the originator is compensated but does not control.

- **No platform silent share.** Zero percent of patent revenue flows to Liana Banyan Corporation itself. The platform's operational funding comes from Pedestal Stake sales through Upekrithen LLC (the holding company) — a separate revenue stream, transparent, ring-fenced from patent revenue.

---

## The Herjavec RPA

- **Source:** Half of the Founder-creator 20% slice.
- **Equivalent total:** 10% of patent revenue.
- **Window:** 20 years.
- **Cap:** $10 million total over the 20-year window.
- **Why one-of-a-kind:** Herjavec negotiates an asymmetric position — not common equity, not common revenue share, but a time-bound / capped RPA that exposes him to the upside of platform success without collapsing the IP split architecture. The pattern is explicitly non-replicable — other investors are offered Pedestal Stakes through Upekrithen, not RPAs.

## How to apply

- **In letters and op-eds:** lead with "Platform retains zero silent share" — that's the rhetorical anchor. Skeptics read platform terms looking for the hidden extractive clause; naming its absence explicitly disarms the suspicion.

- **In investor conversations:** pair 60/20/10/10 with the **Cost+20% margin** rule (separate constitutional law). Investors accustomed to valuing platforms via take-rate hear "Cost+20%" and "zero silent IP share" and recognize the math is constrained in ways that conventional VC terms aren't. Frame as feature, not bug.

- **In patent-office or regulator conversations:** the Pedestal Stake + Upekrithen architecture is the legal vehicle for investor exposure. The 60/20/10/10 split governs patent revenue specifically. Two separate mechanisms; don't conflate them.

- **In cooperative / academic conversations:** this is the Mondragon-adjacent design — compensation ratios are constrained, not by moral exhortation but by constitutional lock. Compare favorably to wage-ratio caps at Mondragon and similar.
