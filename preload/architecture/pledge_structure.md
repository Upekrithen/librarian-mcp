## TL;DR

A three-part structured filing and licensing pattern applied to cooperatively-developed infrastructure: **(1) Defensive filing** — the cooperative corporation files patents on cooperatively-developed infrastructure, establishing priority in friendly hands and preventing patent trolls from filing the same territory. **(2) Perpetual cooperative license** — immediately upon filing, the corporation grants a perpetual, royalty-free, non-sublicensable license to all current and future cooperative members, to individuals/small-businesses/nonprofits/educational-institutions using the invention for non-extractive purposes, and to any platform meeting published cooperative criteria. The license is written into the corporation's bylaws and cannot be revoked by future ownership change, board decision, or restructuring. **(3) Defensive enforcement reserved** — the corporation reserves the right to enforce the patent against extractive commercial use, against any entity asserting any patent against the cooperative or its members (patent retaliation clause), and against any attempt to weaponize the invention against the cooperative movement.

## Independent Claim

**Claim 1.** A computer-implemented and legal-architecture method for cooperatively filing and licensing patents on infrastructure developed by a cooperative platform corporation, comprising:

(a) Filing patents, by a cooperative platform corporation holding title to the patents, on infrastructure developed through cooperative member contribution;

(b) Granting, concurrent with each patent filing, a perpetual royalty-free non-sublicensable license to a defined licensee class comprising: (i) current and future cooperative members; (ii) individuals, small businesses, nonprofits, and educational institutions using the patented invention for non-extractive purposes; (iii) platforms meeting a published set of cooperative criteria including member ownership, Cost+20% or better margin cap, and absence of advertising surveillance;

(c) Codifying the license grant in the cooperative corporation's bylaws such that the license cannot be revoked by any future ownership change, board decision, merger, acquisition, or corporate restructuring;

(d) Reserving defensive enforcement rights against: (i) extractive commercial use that does not meet the cooperative criteria of (b)(iii); (ii) any entity that asserts any patent against the cooperative platform or any cooperative member (patent retaliation clause); (iii) any entity attempting to weaponize the patented invention against the cooperative movement generally;

(e) Including a license-termination clause such that if a licensee sues the cooperative corporation or any cooperative member over any patent, the licensee's perpetual cooperative license under this framework terminates immediately.

**Dependent Claim 1.1** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the cooperative criteria of (b)(iii) include explicit enumerated requirements comprising member ownership of the platform, a margin cap of Cost+20% or more favorable to members, and absence of advertising-based surveillance monetization.

**Dependent Claim 1.2** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the defensive enforcement of (d)(ii) is structured as a license-termination clause (patent retaliation) rather than as a proactive enforcement action, such that the cooperative corporation does not need to initiate litigation in order to invoke the defensive protection.

**Dependent Claim 1.3** — The method of Claim 1, further comprising integration with commercial licensing (per Innovation #2249 Section 9 of Paper #41), such that licensees paying commercial license fees for specific patent families (e.g., ROM-first AI inference) operate under a separate commercial license that does not conflict with the cooperative perpetual license for non-extractive members.

**Dependent Claim 1.4** — The method of Claim 1, wherein the cooperative license grant is implemented through bylaw amendment, board resolution, and a publicly-posted license text that cooperative members and qualifying licensees may reference and rely on.

**Dependent Claim 1.5** — A system comprising a patent portfolio, a corporate bylaw instrument, a publicly-posted license text, a commercial licensing layer, and the defensive enforcement mechanism of Claim 1 through 1.4.

## Prior Art Distinction

Tesla's 2014 "All Our Patent Are Belong To You" pledge, Google's 2013 Open Patent Non-Assertion Pledge, Red Hat's Patent Promise, the Open Invention Network, and the Apache Foundation patent grant provisions all represent prior art in the defensive patent pledge category. **None of these combine (a) cooperative platform application specifically, (b) perpetual cooperative license codified in corporate bylaws rather than in discretionary policy, (c) enumerated cooperative criteria as the licensee qualification gate, (d) integration with a separate commercial licensing layer for non-cooperative licensees, and (e) extension of the platform cooperativism framework to IP strategy as a third axis alongside Scholz's ownership and labor axes.** The combination is novel in cooperative platform architecture and represents a genuine extension of platform cooperativism into the IP governance domain.
